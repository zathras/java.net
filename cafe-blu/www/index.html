Cafe-blu project home page

Cafe-blu is a meeting place for BD-J specification issue discussions. This project is open to BD-J specification experts, device manufacturers and content developers.

Get in touch with the experts, exchange information, share example code, find out what works and what doesn't in BD-J.

Discussion topics will include (but should not be limited to) specification interpretation issues, content authoring, device issues and conformance testing. It's usually bad form to mention a specific player by name, though, and it might violate the terms of your NDA.

Welcome!

Welcome to the Cafe-blu project!

You may to subscribe to the mailing list, browse the archives by clicking the nav bar links.

Note that access to these pages is by invitation of one of the major studios. Access is futher restricted to companies that have valid access to the Blu-ray specifications, part 3. This includes BDA board members, BDA contributor members, BDA FLLA licensees, and companies that have signed the BDA information agreement, and purchased a copy of the 3-2 specification. This forum is for companies and individuals that are developing BD-J related products, or are exploring the possibility of doing business with the format.

Primarily, this group consists of the BD-J discussion forum. You can get to that by clicking "forums" on the left, or directly here. There's also an issue tracking database. Sun may use this to more formally track specific specification issues. If anyone else would like to do that too, we'd welcome it, but in that case please assign the issue to yourself. When discussions reveal a spec issue, Sun (Michael or Bill) will endeavor to add them to the database, and find the best path for communicating them to 2/8.

Membership Policies

Because the membership of this group is restricted, it's necessary that we make a good faith effort to ensure that access really is restricted to people who have a legitimate copy of the BD-ROM spec, part 3. To that end, we ask each company to identify a primary contact. Once a month, that primary contact will be asked to verify that all of the accounts under that company are still valid for current employees.

Anonymous postings are OK. If you'd like to post anonymously, please make up a java.net ID that doesn't identify you or your company, and have your company's primary contact ask us (at bill.foote@sun.com) to add that user ID into the project. I won't disclose the mapping of user IDs to company, even within Sun.
